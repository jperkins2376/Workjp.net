Robert Smalls

From Slave to Congress Man

Robert Smalls was an enslaved African American who became a politician,

in both the South Carolina legislature and the U.S. House of Representatives.

The Time Line of Robert Smalls

  • Born in 1839, in the city of Beaufort, South Carolina.
  • Mother was Lydia Polite, and enslaved woman to Henry Mckee and likely his father.
  • As a slave he worked on a Confederate military transport during the U.S. Civil war.
  • In May 12 1862 Smalls and others enslaved crewmen on the transport stole it and saved their families.
  • From 1862-1865 Smalls helped the Union rescue enslaved families, participate in scrimishes, setting mines and running suplies.
  • After the Civil war went into Business and learn to read and write over a nine month period.
  • In 1874 Smalls was elected to the House of Representives after many years of being in state politics.
